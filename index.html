Positive vs Negative Area

Hello everyone! Today we will learn about finding the area under a curve. In geometry, area is always positive. But in Calculus, area has a direction!

When a graph is above the x-axis, we call it Positive Area. It represents adding something, like money earned or distance traveled forward.

The Flip Side: Negative Area

But what happens when the graph dips below the line?

When the graph is below the x-axis, Calculus treats it as Negative Area. Think of this as losing money, or walking backwards. The math naturally subtracts this value.

Member 3 (2:00 - 3:00)

Crossing the Line: Net Area

Many graphs cross the x-axis. This gives us both positive and negative parts.

Net Area is what you get when you add them together. If you have +5 above the line and -3 below the line, your Net Area is just 2. They cancel each other out!

Member 4 (3:00 - 4:00)

An Easy Example

Let's look at a simple straight line: y = x from -2 to 2.

Area Above (0 to 2) = +2
Area Below (-2 to 0) = -2

Net Area calculation: (+2) + (-2) = 0.

Even though there is physical space colored in, the Net Area is zero because the positive and negative sides are perfectly balanced!

Member 5 (4:00 - 5:00)

Why Does This Matter?

Why do we care about Net Area?

Imagine a battery charging and discharging. The positive area is power going in, the negative area is power going out. The Net Area tells you exactly how much power is left in the battery right now.
